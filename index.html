    About this screener

    This tool implements the Mini-EAT framework developed by Lara-Breitinger et al. (2023) at Mayo Clinic, published in the Journal of the American Heart Association. The original 9-item screener was validated against a 156-item food frequency questionnaire (VioScreen) in 661 adults, achieving a correlation of r = 0.71 with the Healthy Eating Index 2015.

    The validated cutoff scores are: >69 = healthy diet (sensitivity 0.85, specificity 0.73); <61 = unhealthy diet (sensitivity 0.73, specificity 0.87). Item weights reflect the published regression coefficients from the validation study.

    Limitations: validated primarily in a US clinical population (76% women, mean age 36, predominantly college-educated). Results may not generalise equally across all demographic groups. This screener assesses overall diet quality pattern — it does not diagnose individual nutrient deficiencies. Fruit juice is included in the fruit item consistent with HEI-2015 definitions; current 2020–2025 guidelines encourage whole fruit over juice where possible.
